Phishing Awareness Lesson

This is a simulation of a phishing attempt for educational purposes. In a real situation:

  • A legitimate bank would never ask for your full PIN via a website
  • Banks don't typically send urgent account suspension notices via SMS
  • Check the URL - real bank websites use secure domains (https://)
  • Notice pressure tactics creating urgency to make you act without thinking
  • Poor grammar or design can be a red flag in some phishing attempts

If you receive a suspicious message about your bank account:

  1. Don't click on links in the message
  2. Contact your bank directly using the official number on your card
  3. Report suspicious messages to your bank's fraud department
